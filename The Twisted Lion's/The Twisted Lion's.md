# The Twisted Lions
## A Kingdom Divided

In a kingdom where two lions fought for the crown to rule the animal kingdom, destiny took a strange turn. Both lions eventually died, but something magical and mysterious lingered, shaping the fate of the animals that remained.

One lion, chosen as the ruler, was called the **Clown** — not because of weakness, but because of the immense pressures he faced while wearing the crown. The other lion, restless and ambitious, longed to be king. He chose a path that sought an audience, building his power not from strength but from performance — an actor in the kingdom's grand play.

Their father had once dreamed of a better future for them both, a movement of hope and balance. Yet the second lion wanted the kingdom for himself. Some believed his ambition was good, but in reality, not every lion could hold power. Their co-partners, allies, and rivals shaped their struggles, and each lion had to face challenges alone.

One lion tolerated hardship, building his rule with resilience. The other sought popularity, fighting for recognition. The kingdom itself was divided — half in conflict, half in friendship. At times, peace and love smoothed the rule, but the restless lion grew bored. He wanted control, to reshape society in his own image.

---

The animals faced a choice:

- Should they follow the lion who built his kingdom through **strength and endurance**?
- Or the actor-lion, who gained influence through **performance and persuasion**?
- Or perhaps, should they create a **third lion** — a balanced ruler who could unite both paths?

In the end, the actor-lion chose to follow the built one's father, honoring his vision. And so, the possibility of a third lion — a symbol of balance — emerged.

---

*As for me, I choose the built one. Whoever he is, he has earned the right to rule on his own. Perhaps, in the end, the fantastic may happen, and he will win.*
