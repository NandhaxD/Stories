# 🌙 THE DREAM TRAVELER

> *Written by NandhaXD*

---

> **Logline:** A reclusive mathematician discovers he can rewrite his past through his dreams, only to find that every "correction" comes with a terrifying price — and a reality he no longer recognizes.

---

## I. The Awakening

**Sung** is a brilliant but isolated freelance mathematician and software developer. While working from home, exhausted by logic and code, he falls into a deep sleep.

In the void, an *"Unpredictable Face"* — a shifting, shadowy entity — appears.

It whispers a chilling message:

> *"You are the chosen one."*

When Sung wakes, everything seems normal. He returns to his programming — but the line between reality and sleep begins to blur. He suddenly **"passes out"** and finds himself in the past.

---

## II. The First Travels

### 🧠 The Memory of Her

Sung finds himself in a past conversation with his **ex-girlfriend.**

To check if it is real, he reaches out to touch her hand. *The warmth is undeniable.* She looks at him, confused, asking why he is acting so strange.

Sung realizes he is reliving a conversation he already had — but this time, he can *speak differently.*

Suddenly, his mother knocks him on the head. He was actually **asleep on a bench** in the present. His remote job has changed; his company now requires him to work in a physical office.

---

### 📋 The Office Suspension

While at his new office, Sung falls into a trance again.

He is transported to his **previous job** — the one he lost due to family problems and mistakes. He relives the moment his manager hands him a **suspension letter.**

Sung stands frozen, looking like he has seen a ghost.

> *"Why are you stuck? Seen a ghost? Talk!"* — his manager mocks him.

In the real world, his current supervisor screams at him for **sleeping on the job.**

---

## III. The Butterfly Effect 🦋

Sung meets his old girlfriend at a hotel. As they talk about how their lives turned out, she reveals something shocking:

> **She remembers the "new" past he just created.**

Realizing he has the power to truly change his life, Sung goes back to his **university years.** He retakes his entrance exams in his dream, using his future knowledge to pass with the highest marks.

He wakes up in a **new future:**
- ✅ A high-paying, prestigious job
- ✅ Success and recognition
- ✅ Happiness — *or so he thinks*

He looks at his contacts. He is **married** — but not to the girl he loved.

> By changing his academic success, he changed the entire timeline of **who he met and loved.**

---

## IV. The Nightmare Reality 😨

The more Sung travels, the more *"terrifying"* the dreams become.

The people inside his dreams begin to act strangely. They **stop following the script of the past** and look directly at him — knowing he is an intruder. Some even try to **kill him within the dream** to prevent him from ever waking up.

Sung eventually confronts the **"God"** — the creepy creature from the very start. He asks why this power was given to him.

The creature answers:

> *"To let you fulfill your dreams."*
>
> *"But even dreams have bad events. Perfection is a lie."*

---

## V. Conclusion

Exhausted and terrified of the distorted reality he created, Sung **begs the creature to take the power back.**

He realizes that a life without mistakes *isn't life at all.*

Through a final, grueling **mental battle within his own subconscious**, Sung rejects the easy fixes of the "Dream Traveler" — and wakes up in his **original, messy reality.**

He decides to fix his life through:

- 💪 **Hard work**
- 🤝 **Genuine human connection**

*And finally — he finds peace.*
