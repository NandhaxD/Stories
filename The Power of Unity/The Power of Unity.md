
# The Power or the Unity

## The Father and His Sons
Long ago, there was a wise king who had two sons. He wanted to teach them an important lesson about life, leadership, and the true meaning of strength.  
So, he gave them a challenge: to discover whether **power** or **unity** was greater.

---

## The First Son: Power
The first son believed that power was everything.  
He thought:

> "If I command, people will obey. If I desire something, I can take it. With power, I can rule over all."

And so he ruled with authority. His people feared him, respected him, and followed his orders.  
Yet, behind the obedience, his people suffered. They lived in poor conditions, burdened by his endless demands.  
Though he enjoyed pleasure and honor, his rule was built on fear and selfish desires.

---

## The Second Son: Unity
The second son believed in unity.  
He thought:

> "Without my people, I am nothing. A true leader must walk with them, face struggles together, and share both pain and joy."

He worked alongside his people, guiding them through challenges.  
Some disliked him for no reason, but most admired his kindness and strength.  
He was not above them — he was among them. His leadership was built on trust, respect, and shared purpose.

---

## The Father's Question
When the two sons returned home, the father asked them what they had learned.

- The first son said:  
  *"Power gives me everything. I command, and things are done second son said:  
  *"Unity is true power. Together, we achieve more. I gave my people good leadership and showed them the right path."*

The father asked:  
*"Which path will lead to a lasting kingdom? The path of fear and selfish power, or the path of unity and shared strength?"*

The answer was left for the people — and for us — to decide.

---

## Moral of the Story
- **Power alone** may bring quick results, but it fades with time and leaves behind emptiness.  
- **Unity** builds strength that lasts, even through daily challenges.

So, dear reader, who do you think was right?  
The son who ruled with power and died with false gratitude, or the son who led with unity and built a future for his people?
